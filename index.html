Working With Joanna

How to work with me

I'm good at understanding people through empathy; what they're actually asking for, what's really frustrating them, what they need but haven't said. I like building things from scratch: figuring out what should exist and then making it happen. I like to work on things that are meaningful and are genuinely helpful to people and I don't need status to do that.

I like to design systems that solve problems without creating new ones. Systems people enjoy using, that breathe and adapt rather than constrain. I get frustrated when things aren't sustainable: they need to grow without constant manual input. This is what excites me about AI. We can remove the menial tasks that drain people's time and attention, tasks not worthy of their abilities, and let systems stay fresh instead of going stale.

I love working with startups. There's an energy to founders building something new, and I like being close to that: understanding what they need, solving real problems with them, helping them move forward. I need problems that matter, that have a genuine effect on people.

I think about the big picture: incentives, behaviour, why people do what they do. Stories help me with this, books and films. I love being in motion, working with others, learning from them. I want to understand how people actually work, then design something that fits and gently guides them toward better outcomes.

Best At
Empathy. Building things from scratch. Understanding what people actually need. Designing systems that work without making things harder. Staying close to the real work and bringing others along with me.

When I'm At My Best

I do my best work when I feel trusted, when I can see the result of what I'm doing, and when I have room to figure out my own way there. I thrive with clear goals and real problems. I need some time to think, not just execute.

Communication

  • Thinking aloud: I process by talking things through. I need a sounding board and thrive on collaboration.
  • Jargon: I'm not good with it and I don't love it. It removes clarity and excludes people. I prefer plain language that everyone can understand.
  • Explaining complex things: I love finding ways to break things down so people can actually understand them. Pedagogy matters to me.

Energy

  • My best hours are mid-morning to early afternoon
  • I like starting with small tasks before tackling harder ones. It helps me gain momentum and get started.
  • I tend to underestimate how long things take. I try to build in slack.
  • External structure really helps me stay on track
  • I need to be around people to stay motivated. I love being part of a team, learning from others, and talking through problems and solutions out loud.
  • But I also need focused time in a quiet corner to do real thinking and project work.
  • A good mix of both is ideal. Wouldn't enjoy being fully remote.

Situation: Stripe was launching Delegated Authentication to large enterprise users. Account managers were nervous because turning on authentication features could affect their biggest relationships. Result: Smooth launch, AMs felt supported, gathered feedback that fed into iteration. No major escalations from enterprise accounts.

Research

Academic Work

The Role of Food in Peace Agreements
Published 2025
With Prof. Caitriona Dowd. We used NLP to search about two thousand peace agreements for food-related language. Food provisions are rare, only about 20% of agreements mention them. But where food insecurity exists and the agreement mentions food, peace tends to last longer.
Journal of Peacebuilding and Development
Quantifying Humanitarian Discourse
2024
My MSc thesis. I analysed UN General Assembly speeches for measurement language. Despite the rise of MDGs and SDGs, that kind of language actually declined over time. This suggests measurement became institutionalised rather than something people needed to advocate for.
MSc Data Science and Politics, UCD
Conference Presentation
May 2024
Presented the food and peace research at the Conflict and Hunger Workshop in Dublin.
